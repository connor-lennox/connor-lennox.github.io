About Me

I am currently a Master's student at the University of New Hampshire studying under Professor Laura Dietz. I am also a member of the Text Retrieval, Extraction, Machine Learning and Analytics (TREMA) lab at UNH. My work revolves mostly around Natural Language Processing, with a specific interest in text generation. Specifically, my focus is in query-centric text generation, which is distinct from other text generation tasks as the goal is to write text that answers a question or otherwise provides information.

Outside of research, I work on programming projects that are relevant to my personal interests. I've been a musician for most of my life, and used this as inspiration for my undergraduate thesis, in which I used machine learning to write classical music. Recently, I've been learning Rust by creating emulators for the CHIP-8 and Gameboy DMG. In the past, I have participated in a few gamejams, where participants get somewhere between 2-3 days to make a game from scratch.
